Dear Zerin

Buddies are people who you monitor for changes in presence
information.

Watchers are people who monitor YOUR presence information.

Therefore you can add buddies but not watchers. What you can do,
however, is authorise or block watchers from subcribing to your
presence information. This is where the XDMS server comes in. Some
presence servers are able to use an XML presence authorisation
document to decide who may or may not subscribe to your presence. You
must upload this document (pres_rules.xml) from the XDMS interface.
The document can be updated from the watchers tab in the client or
manually from a text editor.

The default setting on most presence servers is to allow watchers by
default so you will probably need to change the presence server
cofiguration to support watcher authorisation.

Hi Zerin,

With only this information it is difficult to diagnose your problem -
however here are the basic steps you need to follow to use the
presence service.

1.    You need to have a presence server enabled (We use OpenSER with 
its presence module - I notice that OpenSER has become OpenSIPS. see
http://www.opensips.org/index.php?n=Resources.DocsPapPa for presence
server info.)

2.    You need to configure iFC in the FHoSS web interface to forward 
all presence related (SUBSCRIBE and PUBLISH messages with event -
presence) to the address and port of your running presence server.

3.    Then you can register Alice and Bob (both with presence enabled)

and publish their status' under the presence tab.

4.    Add bob as Alice's buddy (and vice versa) and note that when new

status' are published, buddies are notified.

If you have followed all these steps and still have problems, post a
full wireshark trace.

Regards,
Richard.
